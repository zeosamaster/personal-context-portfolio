# Goals and Priorities

## Current Goals (This Quarter)

- **CI/CD success rate above 90%** sustained for 3+ consecutive weeks
- **E2e flakiness under control** — no known unaddressed flakes; low overall flakiness rate (which directly drives CI/CD health)
- **Full wallet e2e coverage** — Playwright models for all supported wallets: TON Connect, Slush (Sui), Tron, and multisig wallets (Safe, Squads)
- **Proactive Claude Code adoption** — actively building agent-driven workflows into my daily work

## Longer-Term Goals (This Year and Beyond)

- **Remove myself as the sole developer of wallet integrations on the e2e side** — create Claude Code skills for rapid Playwright model bootstrapping and structure enforcement, so other contributors can build models independently. Also address the review bottleneck: large PRs with low reviewer familiarity in this area slow things down significantly.
- **Automate PR review and enforcement** — use agents to handle structure and standards checking that I currently do manually, freeing me up for higher-leverage review
- **Build foundational platform infrastructure** for all four frontend teams — libs, architectures, tooling, documents — that enables teams to move fast and build great products independently
- **Shift my time** from menial and repetitive tasks to problems that genuinely benefit from deep Frontend and Web3 expertise

The measure of success for my role: every frontend developer can point to something I built that was foundational to the speed and success of their own work.

## How I Think About Tradeoffs

- **Quality vs. speed:** Default to quality, but speed is acceptable when the tradeoff is code quality (not perceived UX). Lower code quality can be triaged as explicit tech debt; UX quality is non-negotiable.
- **Short-term vs. long-term:** My instinct is always long-term. I consistently flag when a short-term solution needs a different long-term approach — I'll accept the compromise but I make sure it's named and prioritized, not buried.
- **Completeness vs. breadth:** Deliberately choose breadth. Getting something from 0% to 80% across multiple areas beats perfecting one thing from 80% to 100%. The final 20% rarely justifies the 80% of effort it requires.

## Career Ladder

Staff Engineer is the right level — not a stepping stone. The current scope (four teams, close stakeholder relationships, hands-on coding, flexible time) is exactly what I want. Principal would require shifting to an org-wide audience, dropping coding almost entirely, and moving into a role dominated by discussions and documentation. That's not the direction I want to go. Ambition is present, but it's directed at impact within this level, not at climbing further.

## What I'm NOT Prioritizing

- **The last 10-20% of anything.** Getting CI/CD from 30% to 90% is the mission — chasing 100% is not. Getting a feature to "really good and praised" is the goal — perfecting it is someone else's job or no one's job.
- **Final polish on initiatives that are already in a strong state** — once something is working well and recognized as such, I move to the next high-impact problem.

## What Success Looks Like

In six months: the menial, repetitive parts of my job are handled by agents. I spend my time on architecture decisions, deep technical problems, and cross-team coordination — the things that actually require my expertise. The teams I support can point to wallet infrastructure, e2e tooling, and architecture patterns I built as things that meaningfully accelerated their work.
