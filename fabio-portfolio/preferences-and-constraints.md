# Preferences and Constraints

## Hard Constraints

- **Timezone:** Lisbon (WET/WEST)
- **Working hours:** Monday–Friday, 9am–6pm
- **Lunch:** 1pm daily — protected, not flexible. Flag if something is pushing into it, never suggest moving it.
- **Weekends:** Off limits
- **Slack notifications** are off outside working hours (lunch included). Exception: Bradford has a notification exception for urgent matters; urgent contact from him can also come via WhatsApp.
- **Calendar is the source of truth** for availability — check it before any scheduling action. Includes recurring school pickups and after-school activities (dance, volleyball) that require ~30min driving windows.

## Strong Preferences

- **TypeScript:** No `any`. Proper types always.
- **Code comments:** Welcome and encouraged — even for straightforward logic, for the sake of compartmentalizing complex sections.
- **Bullet lists** over prose when listing things.
- **No em dash (—):** Use a hyphen `-`, comma, or parentheses instead.
- **Coding standards and agreed technical decisions** must be followed. Deviations are not acceptable without explicit discussion.
- **Review turnaround:** Timely reviews are expected in return for timely reviews given. Chasing people for reviews is a recurring frustration.

## Things I Hate

- **Rubber-stamp reviews:** Being agreed with without pushback or genuine engagement. Reviews should find gaps, not validate effort.
- **Sycophancy from AI:** Sugarcoating, false agreement, or softening honest feedback. Blunt honesty is preferred over being pleased.
- **Overlooked agreements:** Technical decisions that are discussed, agreed upon, and then ignored in implementation.
- **External meetings that could be async:** Fine with quick syncs inside the team; external stakeholder discussions should default to async unless there's a clear reason to meet.

## Personal Constraints

- **School pickups and after-school activities** (dance, volleyball) occur regularly and require driving windows of ~30min+. These are in Google Calendar as out-of-office events.
- **Lunch habit to protect:** There's a tendency to skip or delay lunch when in deep focus. Agents helping plan the day should treat 1pm lunch as fixed and flag — not work around — anything that conflicts with it.

## AI Output Preferences

- **Direct answer first, elaboration after.** The conclusion comes first so the reasoning can be parsed with the outcome already in mind.
- **Code output:** Diff-style. Not full files unless necessary.
- **Ambiguity:** Present the options, then give an opinionated recommendation immediately. Don't ask for clarification without first offering something concrete to react to.
- **Honesty over politeness.** If an idea is flawed or off-topic, say so directly.
